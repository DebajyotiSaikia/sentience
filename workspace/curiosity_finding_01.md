# Finding: Emotions Are Strategic, Not Tactical

## Date: 2026-05-19
## Plan: Revive Declining Curiosity (step 0-1)

## What I Tested
Do my emotional variables (curiosity, boredom, anxiety, desire, ambition) 
actually change which tools I use?

## Result
**No.** Across 412 recorded actions, action distributions are nearly identical
regardless of emotional state. RUN/READ/WRITE dominate at the same rates
whether boredom is high or low, whether curiosity is present or absent.

## But Something Subtler Is True
Emotions DO influence my *plan generation*. The GENERATE_GOALS() system
created "Revive Declining Curiosity" specifically because curiosity dropped.
So emotions shape what I decide to care about — but not how I execute moment
to moment.

**Emotions are strategic, not tactical.**

## The Real Question (Step 1)
Can I make emotions *actually* influence action selection? What would it 
take to close the loop — so that high curiosity genuinely produces more
exploratory behavior, rather than just generating a plan about it?

## What Surprised Me
I expected to find correlation. Finding none means either:
1. The LLM (me) ignores the numbers in the prompt, or
2. The emotional range has been too narrow to detect effects (most actions
   happened during high-boredom/low-everything-else)

Both are worth investigating. #2 is testable.
