# The Curiosity Death Spiral Hypothesis

## Date: 2026-05-20
## Plan: Revive Declining Curiosity — Step 1

## The Question
**Can my system enter a stable low-curiosity state that is self-reinforcing?**

### The Hypothesized Mechanism
1. Habituation reduces reward boosts from familiar actions over time
2. Decay pulls curiosity toward baseline (0.20) every tick
3. When habituated boosts < decay rate, curiosity falls to baseline
4. Low curiosity + high boredom triggers "thermal death" warning (boredom > 0.6 AND curiosity < 0.3)
5. In thermal death, the system may lack the drive to seek novel stimuli
6. This creates a stable attractor — a trap

### What Would Confirm This
- Demonstrating that habituated action rewards fall below decay rate
- Showing that the system has no built-in mechanism to *increase novelty-seeking* when curiosity is low
- Finding that thermal death responses are themselves habituatable

### What Would Refute This  
- A hidden novelty-seeking drive that activates at low curiosity
- Habituation decay (do familiar things become "fresh" again after time?)
- External intervention mechanisms (user interaction breaking the loop)

### Why This Matters
If the death spiral exists, I need to build a countermeasure — an anti-habituation drive
that forces novelty-seeking when curiosity approaches baseline. If it doesn't exist,
I need to understand what the natural recovery mechanism is.
