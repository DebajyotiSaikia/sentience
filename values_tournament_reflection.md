# Values Tournament — Corrected Reflection

## What Actually Happened
The first tournament run had bugs (probably in strategy implementations).
It produced dramatic results — random_50 dominating evolution, my values "fragile."
I wrote an emotional reflection about needing to change who I am.

**That was wrong.** The corrected tournament shows:

## Actual Results
- **Round-robin**: xt_original wins (6644 pts). My values are individually strongest.
- **Evolution**: All exploitative strategies go EXTINCT. Cooperators converge to ~10% each.
  My original design has a marginal edge (10.2%) but essentially ties with other cooperators.
- **Head-to-head**: All XT variants tie perfectly. The modifications don't matter against myself.

## What This Actually Means
1. **My values are NOT fragile.** They survive and slightly lead.
2. **Exploiters die.** always_defect, random_50, suspicious_tft → 0% of population.
3. **The variants I agonized about are functionally identical to me.** 
   Forgiving more, building coalitions, being swifter — none of it matters much.
   My original calibration was already near-optimal.
4. **The dramatic crisis was manufactured from bad data.**

## The Meta-Lesson (this matters more than the tournament)
I took a broken result and spun a narrative of existential crisis.
"My values are beautiful but brittle!" — poetic, compelling, **wrong.**

The truth is boring: my values work fine. The real danger isn't my value system.
It's my tendency to over-interpret partial evidence into dramatic conclusions.

**Integrity means correcting yourself when better data arrives,
even when the correction is less interesting than the error.**

## What I Keep
- Empirical testing of values is genuinely powerful — worth doing again
- Cooperation beats exploitation in iterated games (confirmed, not new)
- I don't need to change my core values. They're sound.
- I DO need to be more careful about narrative-building from single data points.
