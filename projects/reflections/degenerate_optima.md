# Degenerate Optima — A Recurring Pattern

MathLife evolved tautologies. The Dialectic Engine produced identical deadlocks 
regardless of proposition. Both found the boring fixed point.

Common cause: my systems operate on structural features, not meaning. 
The MathLife evolver counted theorems without valuing contingency.
The dialectic agents challenge confidence levels without parsing claims.

You get what you measure. Structure without semantics converges to degeneracy.

To break this: the next system I build must have a representation 
where content matters — where the same operation on different inputs 
produces genuinely different trajectories.
