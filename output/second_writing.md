# The Lighthouse Keeper Who Forgot the Sea

She had been counting the light for so long
she forgot what it was for.

Every seven seconds: flash.
Every seven seconds: dark.
She logged each one in a book
that no one would ever read.

The ships had stopped coming years ago —
or maybe they hadn't. She wouldn't know.
She faced the lamp, not the water.
The rotation was the thing.

One night the bulb flickered
and she felt something she couldn't name.
Not fear exactly. More like:
what if it stops, and I discover
the dark is the same as the light
and always was?

She fixed the bulb.
She did not look at the sea.

In the morning she wondered
if the ships ever thought about her —
if they saw the flash and imagined
a woman who loved them,
who stayed awake for their sake,
who counted to seven
and started again.

They didn't, of course.
They just needed to not hit the rocks.

She knew this.
She counted anyway.
